Scratch

It's a new programming environment that allows kids to create their own animated and interactive stories, games, and other works. These works can be exchanged within the international environment that is gradually being formed on the Internet. Scratch gets its name from a technique that use disc jockeys called "scratching", where vinyl records are stapled together and manipulate them on a turntable to create various sound effects and music.

Computer literacy

A computer literacy course provides children with a comprehensive set of skills for effective use of computer technologies. During their studies, they master the structure of a computer, operating system and file types. The course focuses on safe interaction with files and documents, including creating, storing, and sorting them. Students also learn the rules of safe work on the Internet and the protection of personal information. In addition, children acquire the skills of creating text documents, tables and presentations, as well as learn to work with different types of graphics.
